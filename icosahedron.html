Introduction

An Icosahedron is a twenty sided polygon with equalateral triangle faces, most commonly reffered to by us as a twenty sided die or D20. In another universe it is a whole planet formed of twenty unique planes/environments wherein the actions and destiny of all living things are determined by the roll of their own personal internal icosahedron. On planet Icosa, A "20" results in great rewards, exhaulting victories, and bountiful harvests; whilst rolling a "1" can mean suffering, famine, and death...

Plane 20 of Icosa is home to the Crit Castle, currently being corrupted by a foul being known as Feyl. Feyl and it's minions are spreading a plague of "1's" across the planes of Icosahedron, increasing the amount of sorrow and death to all flora and fauna. The Sole is the lowest of these planes and is home to a small village on the brink of destitution, desperate for a savior. Will you venture into The Icosahedron and restore balance to the life of this world?

Framework

The framework for Icosahedron is based off of tabletop role-playing games using a D20 and other dice to determine a random outcome to situations. We were inspired by aspects of Fallout 1 and 2 for the PC where if a players luck was bad enough, anytime they tried to use their weapon, there was a larger chance that the weapon would break, or they would fall down and lose their turn. Let's face it, sometimes we are just unlucky. Sometimes in life we roll a "1" and we have to deal with that when it happens. In Icosahedron, the player suffers consequences when a "1" is rolled including but not limited to: Broken Weapon, Reduced Stats, Loss of Life Points, Loss of Turn; while the opposite is true of rolling a "20". This adds the thrill and frustration of chance with every action. To apply this, we made a chart of "Calamities" and "Enhancements" that can be determined with the roll of a D20.


Calamities and Enhancements carry over and stack each level. Character stats can not go below -2 and cannot go above 8.
D20 Calamities Enhancements

2 Silence: No Abilities Plus 1 to Attack, Magic, Speed, or Charm

4 Equipment Broken: No weapon and Defense = 10 Healing Water: restores 10 Life
5 Attack -1 Weapon Enhancement: D6 -> D8 -> D10 -> D12 -> 2D6 -> 2D8...
6 Speed -1 Ability Enhancement: D6 -> D8 -> D10 -> D12 -> 2D6 -> 2D8...
7 Defense -1 Healing Water: restores 10 Life

9 Charm -1 Ability Enhancement: D6 -> D8 -> D10 -> D12 -> 2D6 -> 2D8...

11 Speed -1 Plus 5 Max Life; Weapon Enhancement: D6 -> D8 -> D10 -> D12 -> 2D6 -> 2D8...
12 Defense -1 Plus 5 Max Life; Ability Enhancement: D6 -> D8 -> D10 -> D12 -> 2D6 -> 2D8...
13 Magic -1 Plus 5 Max Life; Healing Water: restores 10 Life
14 Charm -1 Plus 10 Max Life;
15 Silence: No Abilities Defense + 1
16 Half current life Plus 1 to Attack, Magic, Speed, or Charm

As stated in the introduction, an icosahedron is made up of 20 sides, or planes, and we wondered what it would be like if each plane was essentially it's own country with unique terrain, creatures, and puzzles that connects to 3 other planes with their own unique features. We then created classes to create the creatures to populate these planes. One detail we are striving for is to have all the beings be gender neutral, and so wanted to start with what a general "being" would consist of. We started with the Being class and then branched off from that with Hero and Rival; then created Loot, Food, Weapon, and Ability classes to create objects to populate some of the fields of Heroes and Rivals. Next we created the Cache class which we used to create arrays of objects. Now we can create a Plane class comprised of seperate Caches for Rivals, Loot, Food, Traps, etc., that can be easily populated with as many objects as the Plane builder wants and can then be selected directly or randomly through Math.random like a dice roll. Now we have a framework to create any type of creature we want with any type of stats, along with any type of object to then create as many levels as we want, each populated with unique creatures and objects. Being Class

Being Class JavaScript
class Being {
  constructor(name, title, maxLife, life, attack, defense, magic, speed, charm, weapon, pack) {
    this.name=name; this.title=title; this.maxLife=maxLife; this.life=life; this.attack=attack; this.defense=defense; this.magic=magic; this.speed=speed; this.charm=charm; this.weapon=weapon; this.pack=pack;
  }

  rollD20() {
    let roll = Math.floor(Math.random()*20) +1;
    return roll; 
  }

  plusStat(stat, num) {
    stat = stat + num;
    return stat;
  }

  minusStat(stat, num) {
    stat = stat - num;
    return stat;
  }
  
  attackTarget(target) {
    let attackRoll = this.rollD20();
    let bonus = this.attack;
    let attackNum = attackRoll + bonus;

    if(attackNum <= target.defense) {
      return `${this.name} rolls an attack of: ${attackRoll} + ${bonus}\n which does not overcome ${target.name}'s defenses`;
    } else {
    let weapon = Math.floor(Math.random()*this.weapon) +1;
    
    let damage = weapon + bonus;
    target.life = target.life - damage;
    return `${this.name} rolls an attack of: ${attackRoll} + ${bonus}\nwhich penetrates ${target.name}'s defenses doing ${damage} damage!`;
    }
  }
  search() {
   let searchRoll = this.rollD20() + this.charm;
    if(searchRoll > 10) {
      return "found"
    }
    return searchRoll;
  }

  orderRoll() {
    let orderRoll = this.rollD20() + this.speed;
    return orderRoll;
  }

  consume(loot) {
    let healing = loot.healing;
    if(this.life < this.maxLife) {
      this.life = this.life + healing;
      loot.quantity--;
      if(this.life > this.maxLife) {
        this.life = this.maxLife;
      }
    } else {
      return 'Not Hungry'
    }
  }
}
Name The name of the being
Title States the kind of being it is
Max-Life Stores the current maximum amount of Life the being has.
Life Stores the beings current Life points
Attack The Attack stat number is added to the beings attack rolls and attack abilities.
Defense The Defense stat is the number another being's attack roll must beat to land a hit.
Magic The Magic stat number is added to magic abilities, used to disarm magic traps, and to find magically hidded paths and items.
Speed The Speed stat number is added to the being's order roll which determines the order of combat. It is also used to avoid traps and escape danger
Charm The Charm stat number is added to charm abilities, used for social interactions, and to discover hidden areas and treasure
Weapon The Weapon number indicates what die to use for determining the weapon's damage
Pack The Pack is an empty array inventory used to store items the being finds
rollD20() The RollD20 method uses the javascript Math.random to generate a random number between 1-20
plusStat(stat, num) The plusStat method increases a specific stat by a specific number
minusStat(stat, num) The minusStat method decreases a specific stat by a specific number
orderRoll() The OrderRoll method uses the rollD20 method and then adds the being's Speed stat to it and compares that number to their opponents' orderRoll to determine the order of combat
search() The Search method uses the rollD20 method and then adds the being's Charm stat and compares that to the difficulty of the thing they are searching for
consume(item) The Consume method uses an item and follows the conditions of that item
Hero Class

The Hero class extends the Being class, meaning it has all the fields and methods of Being as well as adding abilities and another method.


Hero Class JavaScript
class Hero extends Being {
  constructor(name, title, maxLife, life, attack, defense, magic, speed, charm, weapon, attackAbility, magicAbility, speedAbility, charmAbility, pack) {
    super(name, title, maxLife, life, attack, defense, magic, speed, charm, weapon, pack)
    this.attackAbility=attackAbility; this.magicAbility=magicAbility;
    this.speedAbility=speedAbility; this.charmAbility=charmAbility;
  }

loot(target) {
  
  for(let i=0; i < target.pack.length; i++) {
    
    if(target.pack[i] === this.pack[i]) {
      this.pack[i].quantity++;
      target.pack.splice(i, 1);
    }if(target.pack[i] !== this.pack[i]){
       this.pack.push(target.pack[i])
       target.pack.splice(i, 1);
    }
    
  }
}
}
Attack Ability Each Hero has an Attack Ability that has it's own damage die and a cooldown for how many turns until the player can use that ability again
Magic Ability Each Hero has a Magic Ability that has it's own damage die and a cooldown for how many turns until the player can use that ability again
Speed Ability Each Hero has a Speed Ability that has it's own damage die and a cooldown for how many turns until the player can use that ability again
Charm Ability Each Hero has a Speed Ability that has it's own damage die and a cooldown for how many turns until the player can use that ability again
loot(target) The loot method allows the Hero to move the items to their pack

  }

  increase(){
this.quantity++;
}
  decrease(){
if(this.quantity > 0) {
  this.quantity--;
  }
}
  
}

Tier 1

  

    The first tier of The Icosahedron is playing through the story of Icosa. The player starts the game at a shabby campsite in The Sole. In one direction they see a dreary farm in the distance next to a foul looking brownish red river. In another direction, they see a dying forest with a plume of smoke coming from the middle. The player makes choices and solves puzzles to help the citizens and access new areas. There are 20 planes in Icosa, and while the player can complete the planes in order from 1 to 20, they are not specifically connected in numerical order. Each plane connects to three other planes, for example: Plane 1: The Sole, connects to Plane 2: The Cave, Plane 5: The Woods, and Plane 8: The Falls. This means the player can access Plane 8, as long as they complete the requirements to do so, from the first Plane. This creates many different paths and options to reach the Crit Castle and save Icosa.

Tier 2

  

    Defeating Feyl and stopping the corruption of Icosa was just the beginning. Now it is time to rule and rebuild. Because of the devistation brought by Feyl, the inhabitants of Icosa were very near extinction and are in need of compassionate leadership. Now the player must send new heroes out into the Planes to collect materials and recruits to start improving and repopulating the Planes as new threats are awakened in the depths of Icosa to challenge the shift of power. Once enough of Icosa is restored, it's secrets are unlocked revealing that this planet is actually a small moon orbiting another world and that the Crit Castle has a mechanism to open a portal to this newly discovered celestial sphere. Now the player can take their hero or create new heroes to team up with other players to explore this new world of Talus in order to prevent the summoning of a being capable of tearing apart the fabric of this universe. This allows players to create specialized heroes with more unique skills to create unique groups of players going on missions together. The design document for this tier of the game is called  I Crit You Not, and I actually wrote it before writing Icosahedron, so some things are the same and others have been changed, but it has the premise laid out to show the foundation. The world of  Talus and the conflicts involved are detailed in a story I created as a dungeon master for a group of friends to play through over the course of two years.
